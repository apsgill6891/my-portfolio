Aerospace Consulting 2022–2024 · Abbotsford, BC
IMP Group — Cascade Aerospace
Consultant · USAF Avionics Upgrade Program · Revenue ~CAD $600M
The Challenge

The USAF avionics upgrade program was running 50% over its turnaround time target — 180 days actual versus a 120-day contractual commitment. Incremental improvements had plateaued. No one had found the root cause, and the $270M NATO contract renewal was at risk.

What I Did
  • Built trust with skeptical stakeholders over time — the data only became accessible once relationships were established
  • Collected stage-by-stage data including non-routines, training hold times, and parts "robbing" across all active aircraft
  • Re-visualized the data as a Gantt chart (instead of standard summary tables) — which revealed that the program was handling 6 aircraft concurrently despite a contractual limit of 4, creating systemic resource contention in final delivery stages
  • Presented the counter-intuitive recommendation to the COO: reduce concurrent aircraft by 2 to reduce overall throughput time — backed by stage-wise data and simulation
  • The COO approved the change; TAT dropped from 180 to 105 days — beating the 120-day target

"Presenting data differently is everything. The bottleneck had been there for months. Nobody saw it until we stopped looking at averages and started looking at overlap."

Safety · Culture Change 2022–2024 · Abbotsford, BC
Cascade Aerospace — Safety Transformation
Consultant · Safety Culture & Audit Recovery
The Challenge

Cascade had ~30 safety incidents that had triggered audit findings. Engineers were going through safety motions but not internalizing the culture. A top-down compliance push was failing. The COO needed a different approach — fast.

What I Did
  • Coached 300+ engineers through hands-on safety demonstrations — not classroom sessions, but floor-level walkthroughs anchored in real scenarios they recognized
  • Persuaded the COO to run a "crowdsource Fix Ideas" campaign — shifting ownership of safety problems from management to the engineers who lived them daily
  • Introduced a "Best Safety Idea of the Month" recognition program to reward proactive engagement rather than just incident-free streaks
  • Shifted the safety conversation from compliance to contribution — turning engineers from rule-followers into problem-solvers

"Safety culture doesn't change through audits — it changes when people feel ownership. The crowdsource campaign gave 300 engineers a voice. The incidents disappeared because the mindset shifted, not because the rules got stricter."

Behavioral Change · KPI Design 2019–2020 · New Delhi, India
Blowhorn — Mobile App Adoption
Operations Manager · 25-city rollout
The Challenge

Mobile app usage was stuck at 38%. The KPI measuring it was binary — used or not used — giving zero insight into where to intervene. Incentives and mandates had failed for over a month. The problem wasn't motivation; it was measurement.

What I Did
  • Identified that the binary KPI was the root problem — it provided no diagnostic value and no reward gradient
  • Collaborated with the data team to redesign the metric into usage tiers: 0–20%, 20–40%, 40–60%, 60–80%, 80%+
  • Implemented tiered incentives and penalties aligned to the new tiers — creating a visible, motivating ladder rather than a binary pass/fail
  • Ran targeted coaching sessions at each tier, addressing specific barriers at each level of adoption
  • The tiered KPI structure became the standard playbook adopted across all ~25 cities

"The app didn't change. The team didn't change. The metric changed. Reframing how you measure a problem is often the same as solving it."

Safety Design · Maritime 2012–2018 · Indian Ocean
MOL Maritime — Piracy Evacuation Protocol
Marine Navigating Officer · High-risk maritime corridor
The Challenge

The ship had undergone structural modifications. The existing piracy evacuation procedure — muster at assembly point, then secure the citadel — required 12 minutes total. In high-risk corridors, 3 minutes can mean the difference between safety and capture.

What I Did
  • Noticed that a new generator installation had created an unintended direct exit route connecting the accommodation block to the cargo decks
  • Evaluated 2 potential escape routes across 6 parameters: distance, accessibility, choke points, visibility, capacity, and muster proximity
  • Calculated that the new route reduced transit time to the citadel by 3 minutes (30% reduction)
  • Presented the analysis to the captain with full documentation — received approval to update the SOP
  • Changes were adopted into standard operating procedures on 3 sister ships initially — later expanding to 6

"Every new ship has a different geometry. Most officers learn the SOP and move on. The question I asked was: given this ship, right now, is this the fastest path? The answer was no."
