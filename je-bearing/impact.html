Before vs After

What changes with decision support

❌

Before — Unstructured Review

🔴Planning decisions live in one person's head — one absence breaks the week
🔴Trade-offs (setup vs inventory, OT vs lateness) are made implicitly, inconsistently
🔴No visibility into operator overload until a machine stops running
⚠️Overtime is authorized reactively, not proactively — always at premium urgency
⚠️New job quoting takes hours because there's no structured "similar parts" lookup
⚠️Setup opportunities (running similar parts back-to-back) are missed every week
✅

After — Structured Decision Support

✅Canonical data objects encode shop knowledge — any planner can run the meeting
✅Trade-offs are visible, quantified, and scenario-tested before a decision is made
✅Operator load scoring flags overload risk 3 days before it becomes a problem
✅OT scenarios are modeled on Monday — decision made with full cost visibility
✅Same/Similar lookup returns comparable quotes in seconds, not hours
✅Setup sequencing recommendations automatically surface consolidation opportunities
